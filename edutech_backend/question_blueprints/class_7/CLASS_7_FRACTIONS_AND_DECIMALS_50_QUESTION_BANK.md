## Question 1
Question Type: Direct Concept
Difficulty Level: Foundation
Question Text: Which of the following is a proper fraction?
Options:
- A. `9/7`
- B. `5/5`
- C. `3/8`
- D. `11/4`
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: A proper fraction has numerator smaller than denominator, so its value is less than 1. `3/8` fits this definition. `9/7` and `11/4` are improper fractions, and `5/5 = 1`, so it is not proper.
Common Mistake: Students often think any fraction written with two numbers is automatically a proper fraction.
Learning Objective: Identify proper fractions from a mixed set of fractions.

## Question 2
Question Type: Direct Concept

Question Text: What is the decimal form of `7/10`?
Options:
- A. `0.07`
- B. `0.7`
- C. `7.0`
- D. `70`
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: A denominator of 10 means the numerator is placed in the tenths position. So `7/10 = 0.7`. The other options place the digit in the wrong place value or change the value completely.
Common Mistake: Confusing tenths with hundredths.
Learning Objective: Convert fractions with denominator 10 into decimals.

## Question 3

Options:

- B. `3/6`
- C. `4/10`
- D. `5/12`
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Equivalent fractions name the same part of a whole. `3/6` simplifies by dividing numerator and denominator by 3 to get `1/2`. The other fractions simplify to different values.
Common Mistake: Looking only for even numbers without checking the actual value.
Learning Objective: Recognize equivalent fractions.

- D. Cannot be compared

- B. `0.7`
- C. `0.75`
- D. `2/3`
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: The midpoint is the average: `(0.6 + 0.8) / 2 = 1.4 / 2 = 0.7`. The other options are not equally distant from `0.6` and `0.8`.
Common Mistake: Picking a number that merely lies between the two values.
Learning Objective: Find the midpoint between two decimals.

## Question 47
Question Type: Multi-Step Reasoning
Difficulty Level: Advanced
Question Text: A container is `5/6` full. `1/4` of the container is emptied. What fraction of the container remains full?
Options:

- B. `2/3`
- C. `11/24`
- D. `3/4`
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: Start with `5/6` full and remove `1/4` of the whole container, not `1/4` of `5/6`. So remaining amount is `5/6 - 1/4 = 10/12 - 3/12 = 7/12`. The other answers come from using the wrong interpretation of “of the container.”

## Question 49

## Question 50
Question Type: Short Answer
Difficulty Level: Advanced
Question Text: A book has `240` pages. Priya has read `3/5` of it and then reads another `0.1` of the whole book. How many pages has she read in all?
Correct Answer: `168 pages`
Explanation: `3/5` of 240 pages is `144` pages. `0.1` of the whole book means `24` more pages. So the total read is `144 + 24 = 168` pages. A common mistake is to calculate `0.1` of the remaining part instead of the whole book.
Common Mistake: Applying the decimal part to the unread pages instead of the total pages.
Learning Objective: Combine fraction and decimal reasoning in a multi-step context.
