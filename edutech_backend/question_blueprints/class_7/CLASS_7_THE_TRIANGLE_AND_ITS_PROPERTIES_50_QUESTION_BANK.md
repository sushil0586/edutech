## Question 1
Question Type: Direct Concept
Difficulty Level: Foundation
Question Text: How many sides does a triangle have?
Options:
- A. 2
- B. 3
- C. 4
- D. 5
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: A triangle is a closed figure made of three line segments, so it has 3 sides. The other options describe different shapes or incomplete figures.
Common Mistake: Confusing the number of vertices with the number of visible corners in a rough sketch.
Learning Objective: Recall the basic definition of a triangle.

## Question 2
Question Type: Direct Concept
Difficulty Level: Foundation
Question Text: A triangle with all sides equal is called:
Options:
- A. scalene
- B. isosceles
- C. equilateral
- D. right-angled
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: An equilateral triangle has all three sides equal. An isosceles triangle has exactly two equal sides, and a scalene triangle has all sides unequal.
Common Mistake: Thinking every triangle with equal sides is isosceles only.
Learning Objective: Identify an equilateral triangle from its side property.

## Question 3

Learning Objective: Recall the angle-sum property of a triangle.

## Question 5
Question Type: Direct Concept

Question Text: A triangle that has one angle of `90 degrees` is called:
Options:
- A. acute-angled triangle
- B. right-angled triangle
- C. obtuse-angled triangle

Correct Answer: B
Explanation: A triangle with one right angle is called a right-angled triangle. Acute and obtuse triangles are classified by different angle measures, while scalene refers to side lengths.
Common Mistake: Looking only at side lengths when the question asks about an angle.
Learning Objective: Identify a right-angled triangle.

## Question 8
Question Type: Direct Concept
Difficulty Level: Foundation
Question Text: Which of the following can form a triangle?

- B. `5 cm, 5 cm, 9 cm`
- C. `1 cm, 2 cm, 3 cm`

Learning Objective: Apply the triangle inequality.

## Question 12

Explanation: Consecutive odd numbers differ by 2, so if the first side is the smallest, the next two must be 2 cm and 4 cm larger than it. The set `5, 7, 9` matches this exactly and also forms a valid triangle.
Common Mistake: Ignoring whether the side set still satisfies triangle inequality after pattern matching.
Learning Objective: Combine pattern recognition with triangle validity.

## Question 49

Question Text: Which statements are always true? Select all that apply.
Options:
- A. The side opposite the largest angle is the longest
- B. An equilateral triangle is acute-angled
- C. Every isosceles triangle is equilateral
- D. A triangle cannot have more than one obtuse angle

Explanation: A is a standard side-angle property, B is true because each equilateral angle is `60 degrees`, and D is true because two obtuse angles would exceed `180 degrees`. C is false.

Learning Objective: Identify universal truths about triangles.

## Question 50
Question Type: Short Answer
Difficulty Level: Advanced
Question Text: A triangle has one angle `78 degrees`, and the other two angles are equal. Find those two angles.
Correct Answer: `51 degrees and 51 degrees`
Explanation: The two equal angles together measure `180 - 78 = 102 degrees`. Since they are equal, each one is `102/2 = 51 degrees`.
Common Mistake: Subtracting `78` from `90` because the angles are equal.
Learning Objective: Solve a short-answer triangle angle problem using equality and angle sum.
