# Health in Adolescence Topic Blueprint

## Topic Identity

- Class: 7
- Subject: Science
- Topic: Health in Adolescence
- Board / Curriculum: CBSE / premier-board compatible
- Intended pack size: 100 questions
- Recommended difficulty mix: 20 foundation, 30 intermediate, 50 advanced

## Why This Topic Needs A Separate Blueprint

This topic becomes repetitive when generation keeps producing:

- identify adolescence by age
- choose a healthy habit
- state one puberty change
- repeated assertion-reason items on growth and hygiene

A premium pack must balance physical growth, nutrition, hygiene, sleep, emotional change, routine analysis, and misconception correction.

## Topic Scope

Include:

- adolescence as a stage of growth
- healthy food and nutrition basics
- exercise and rest
- personal hygiene
- emotional and social changes
- routine and lifestyle choices
- misconception correction

Exclude:

- highly detailed reproductive-system biology
- board-inappropriate medical depth
- counseling-style content beyond age-appropriate academic scope

## Stem Variety Bank

- Two students follow different routines. Which one...
- A student feels tired in class every morning. Which habit is most likely causing this?
- Which statement best corrects the idea that...
- A school counselor reviews the following routine...
- Which change would help this student most?
- Assertion (A)... Reason (R)...

Rule:

- do not let “A student says...” dominate the entire pack
- do not repeat one advice-style scenario with cosmetic changes only

## Context Bank

- school schedule
- sports practice
- exam season
- skipped breakfast
- poor sleep habits
- hygiene after outdoor activity
- mood and social behavior in school

## Anti-Repetition Rules

Hard bans:

- do not create repeated “sleep is important” items with slightly changed wording
- do not create repeated “balanced diet is necessary” items with different names only
- do not let advanced questions collapse into assertion-reason plus one myth pattern
- do not over-repeat puberty-identification questions

Near-duplicate test:

- if two questions recommend the same habit using the same scenario structure, rewrite one with a different reasoning frame

## Difficulty Design

### Foundation

- test recognition of habits, changes, and basic needs
- avoid baby-level obviousness

### Intermediate

- test cause-effect between routine and health outcomes
- include comparisons, school-life contexts, and explanation-based choices

### Advanced

- test prioritization, misconception correction, and multi-factor reasoning
- advanced must involve more than naming one good habit

## Distractor Design Rules

- extreme but tempting health advice
- incomplete routines that seem healthy on the surface
- myths from peers or online tips
- confusion between temporary comfort and healthy long-term behavior

## Banned Weak Patterns

- repeated “choose the healthy habit” lists
- repeated “which nutrient is needed” single-line recall questions
- repeated “stomata / plants” style cross-topic leakage
- visible generator IDs or source labels in question text

## Review Checklist

- at least 15 visibly distinct stems in a 100-question pack
- at least 10 routine-analysis questions
- at least 8 misconception-correction questions
- emotional/social change is represented, not ignored
- advanced questions include prioritization and trade-off reasoning
