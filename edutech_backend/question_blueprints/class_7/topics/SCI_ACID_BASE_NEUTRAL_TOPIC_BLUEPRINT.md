# Acidic, Basic, and Neutral Substances Topic Blueprint

## Topic Identity

- Class: 7
- Subject: Science
- Topic: Acidic, Basic, and Neutral Substances
- Board / Curriculum: CBSE / premier-board compatible
- Intended pack size: 100 questions
- Recommended difficulty mix: 20 foundation, 30 intermediate, 50 advanced

## Why This Topic Needs A Separate Blueprint

This topic often degrades into repetitive content such as:

- litmus turns red or blue
- classify a household item as acid or base
- identify a sour substance

A strong topic pack must also cover indicator behavior, observation logic, experimental reasoning, safety, neutralization, and misconception correction.

## Topic Scope

Include:

- acids, bases, and neutral substances
- indicators and color change
- litmus observations
- turmeric and china rose style indicator logic where appropriate
- household examples
- neutralization situations
- safe handling
- interpretation of simple classroom experiments

Exclude:

- pH scale calculations
- ionic equations
- salt chemistry beyond class-level need
- strong laboratory chemistry not suitable for Class 7

## Stem Variety Bank

- A student tests a liquid with...
- During an activity, the color changes from...
- Which observation best suggests that...
- A household label is missing. Which clue is most useful?
- A child says all acids are dangerous. Which is the best correction?
- A teacher mixes two substances in small quantity and notes...

Rule:

- do not let “Which of the following is acidic?” dominate the set
- do not overuse one litmus sentence pattern

## Context Bank

- kitchen ingredients
- bathroom or cleaning agents
- garden soil treatment
- ant sting or insect-bite discussion
- classroom tray experiment
- lab safety reasoning

## Anti-Repetition Rules

Hard bans:

- no more than 2 very similar litmus-only questions in a 20-question block
- do not create repeated “sour means acid” questions
- do not create repeated “soap is basic” questions without new reasoning
- assertion-reason questions must rotate sub-concepts, not wording only

Near-duplicate test:

- if the student could answer two questions using the same one-line memory hook with only object names changed, rewrite one

## Difficulty Design

### Foundation

- test identification, classification, and simple indicator outcomes
- avoid trivial memorization-only overload

### Intermediate

- test observation interpretation, safe reasoning, and neutralization contexts
- include “best explanation” and “best next step” formats

### Advanced

- test inference from experiment results, misconception correction, and cause-effect logic
- do not reduce advanced level to one repeated assertion-reason template

## Distractor Design Rules

- confusion between acid/base because of taste or touch assumptions
- wrong indicator interpretation
- overgeneralization that all acids burn skin
- confusion between neutral and “safe”
- treating one familiar example as universal
