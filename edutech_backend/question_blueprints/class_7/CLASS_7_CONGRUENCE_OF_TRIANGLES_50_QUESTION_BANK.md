## Question 1
Question Type: Direct Concept
Difficulty Level: Foundation
Question Text: Two figures are congruent if they:
Options:
- A. have the same color
- B. have the same shape and the same size
- C. have the same shape only
- D. have the same area only
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Congruent figures match exactly in shape and size when one is placed over the other. Same shape only describes similarity, and same area alone does not guarantee congruence.
Common Mistake: Thinking figures with equal area must always be congruent.
Learning Objective: Understand the basic meaning of congruence.

## Question 2
Question Type: Direct Concept
Difficulty Level: Foundation
Question Text: If two triangles are congruent, their corresponding sides are:
Options:
- A. parallel
- B. equal
- C. always vertical
- D. always different
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Congruent triangles have equal corresponding sides and equal corresponding angles. The other properties are not required.
Common Mistake: Looking for special positions instead of equal matching parts.
Learning Objective: Recall a key property of congruent triangles.

## Question 3
Question Type: Direct Concept
Difficulty Level: Foundation
Question Text: Which criterion proves congruence of two triangles using all three sides?
Options:
- A. ASA
- B. RHS
- C. SSS
- D. AAS
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: SSS means side-side-side, where all three corresponding sides are equal. That is enough to prove two triangles congruent.
Common Mistake: Mixing SSS with SSA, which is not a standard congruence rule here.
Learning Objective: Identify the SSS congruence criterion.

## Question 4
Question Type: Direct Concept
Difficulty Level: Foundation
Question Text: In the criterion SAS, the letters stand for:
Options:
- A. side-angle-side
- B. side-angle-sum
- C. same-angle-side
- D. side-area-side
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: SAS means two corresponding sides and the included angle are equal. The other expansions are not valid congruence criteria.
Common Mistake: Forgetting that the angle in SAS must be the included angle.
Learning Objective: Expand and interpret SAS correctly.

## Question 5

Difficulty Level: Foundation
Question Text: Which congruence rule is used specially for right triangles?
Options:
- A. SSS
- B. RHS
- C. SAS
- D. ASA
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: RHS stands for right angle-hypotenuse-side and is used for right triangles. The other criteria apply more generally.
Common Mistake: Using RHS for triangles that are not right-angled.
Learning Objective: Recognize the RHS criterion.

## Question 6

Difficulty Level: Intermediate
Question Text: Triangle ABC congruent to triangle DEF. If AB = `9 cm`, BC = `11 cm`, AC = `13 cm`, find the perimeter of triangle DEF.
Options:
- A. `22 cm`
- B. `33 cm`
- C. `31 cm`
- D. `29 cm`
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Congruent triangles have equal corresponding side lengths. So triangle DEF also has sides `9 cm, 11 cm, 13 cm`, and its perimeter is `9 + 11 + 13 = 33 cm`.
Common Mistake: Adding only two corresponding sides.
Learning Objective: Use congruence to find a derived measure.

## Question 46
Question Type: Multi-Step Reasoning
Difficulty Level: Intermediate
Question Text: Two right triangles are congruent by RHS. Their hypotenuse is `15 cm`, and one leg is `9 cm`. What can be said about the corresponding leg in the second triangle?
Options:
- A. It is `6 cm`
- B. It is `9 cm`
- C. It is `15 cm`
- D. It cannot be known
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: Since the triangles are congruent, corresponding sides are equal. Therefore the matching leg in the second triangle is also `9 cm`.
Common Mistake: Trying to recalculate a side that is already given equal by congruence.
Learning Objective: Use established congruence to transfer side measures.

## Question 47
Question Type: Multi-Step Reasoning
Difficulty Level: Advanced
Question Text: In two triangles, AB = PQ, BC = QR, and angle B = angle Q. If angle B lies between sides AB and BC, what conclusion follows?
Options:
- A. The triangles are congruent by SAS
- B. The triangles are congruent by SSS
- C. The triangles are only similar
- D. No conclusion is possible
Correct Answer: A
Explanation: The equal angle is included between the two equal sides in each triangle. That matches the SAS criterion exactly, so the triangles are congruent.
Common Mistake: Missing the importance of the angle being between the equal sides.
Learning Objective: Apply included-angle reasoning carefully.

## Question 49
Question Type: Multi-Select
Difficulty Level: Advanced
Question Text: Which conclusions must follow if two triangles are congruent? Select all that apply.
Options:
- A. Corresponding angles are equal
- B. Corresponding sides are equal
- C. Areas are equal
- D. They must be drawn in the same orientation
Correct Answer: A, B, C
Explanation: Congruent triangles match completely, so corresponding sides, corresponding angles, and area are equal. Orientation on the page does not matter.
Common Mistake: Treating appearance on paper as part of the definition.
Learning Objective: Identify multiple consequences of congruence.

## Question 50
Question Type: Short Answer
Difficulty Level: Advanced
Question Text: Triangle ABC congruent to triangle DEF. If angle D = `48 degrees` and angle E = `67 degrees`, find angle C.
Correct Answer: `65 degrees`
Explanation: Since triangle ABC is congruent to triangle DEF, angle A = angle D = `48 degrees` and angle B = angle E = `67 degrees`. Then angle C = `180 - (48 + 67) = 65 degrees`.
Common Mistake: Forgetting the order of correspondence and matching C with E.
Learning Objective: Use correspondence and angle sum in a short-answer problem.
