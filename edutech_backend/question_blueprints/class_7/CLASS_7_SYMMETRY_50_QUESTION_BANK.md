## Question 1
Question Type: Direct Concept
Difficulty Level: Foundation
Question Text: What is meant by a line of symmetry?
Options:
- A. a line that measures length
- B. a line that divides a figure into two matching halves
- C. a line that always makes a right angle
- D. a line drawn only inside triangles
Correct Answer: B
Explanation: A line of symmetry divides a figure into two parts that match exactly when folded along that line. The other options do not describe symmetry.
Common Mistake: Thinking any line passing through the center is a line of symmetry.
Learning Objective: Define a line of symmetry.

## Question 2
Question Type: Direct Concept
Difficulty Level: Foundation
Question Text: Which of the following has exactly one line of symmetry?
Options:
- A. a scalene triangle
- B. an equilateral triangle
- C. an isosceles triangle
- D. a general parallelogram
Correct Answer: C
Explanation: An isosceles triangle has exactly one line of symmetry through the vertex and midpoint of the base. An equilateral triangle has three, while a scalene triangle and a general parallelogram have none.
Common Mistake: Confusing isosceles and equilateral triangles.
Learning Objective: Identify symmetry in triangles.

Explanation: A non-equilateral isosceles triangle has exactly one line of symmetry and rotational order 1. The other options have much more symmetry.
Common Mistake: Ignoring the phrase “exactly one.”
Learning Objective: Identify a shape from combined symmetry restrictions.

## Question 48

- A. rectangle

## Question 49
Question Type: Multi-Select
Difficulty Level: Advanced
Question Text: Which statements are true? Select all that apply.
Options:
- A. Every square has line symmetry
- B. Every circle has infinitely many lines of symmetry
- C. Every shape with rotational symmetry has line symmetry
- D. A regular pentagon has rotational symmetry
Correct Answer: A, B, D
Explanation: A square has line symmetry, a circle has infinitely many symmetry lines, and a regular pentagon has rotational symmetry. Statement C is false because some figures have rotational symmetry without line symmetry.
Common Mistake: Assuming one kind of symmetry automatically gives the other.
Learning Objective: Evaluate multiple symmetry statements.

## Question 50
Question Type: Short Answer
Difficulty Level: Advanced
Question Text: A regular octagon has rotational symmetry. Find its order and the least angle of rotation.
Correct Answer: `Order = 8, least angle = 45 degrees`
Explanation: A regular octagon has as many rotational positions as it has sides, so the order is 8. Least angle = `360/8 = 45 degrees`.
Common Mistake: Dividing by 4 because the octagon has opposite sides.
Learning Objective: Find both rotational order and least angle for a regular polygon.
